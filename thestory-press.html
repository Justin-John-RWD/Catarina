Wednesday, April 30, 2008: Alcaldia denounces British Embassy in daily tabloid "Nicaragua Hoy" for supposed threat to break off Diplomatic Relations.


British Embassy Threaten Alcaldia of Catarina.
Nicaragua Hoy, April 30, 2008
The Mayor of Catarina refers to a letter he received from the British Ambassador, Kennedy, on April 23rd. Supposedly, the Ambassador threatens that the British Government would break off diplomatic relations with Nicaragua if the Alcaldia of Catarina refuse to accept the Court Rulings. The reporter comments that the letter was not shown but that, however, the Mayor "assured" her (the reporter) of its content and meaning.
The Mayor goes on to suggest that while the Courts may have passed judgment, the Government and Daniel Ortega have not yet passed judgment - an apparent suggestion that this Mayor (and Alcaldia) will only answer to Daniel Ortega! Additionally, and not surprisingly, the Mayor has changed his story again. Apparently, due to the "disappearance" of the Cooperativa, the land has passed (by default?) to the "school". We spoke with the Reporter responsible for this story on May 2nd and she told us that "no documents" were shown and that she reported the "verbal claims" of the Alcalde.


Monday June 16th,2008, National Daily Tabloid, "Hoy": Mr Dobson shows Documents contradicting verbal claims by Alcaldia.


Mr Dobson Responds by Showing all Original Documents and Court Sentences
Nicaragua Hoy, June 16th, 2008
The article mentions that Mr Justin Dobson presented original documents relating to Original Title, Purchase of the Property, Certified Court Sentences in favor of the Cooperativa and himself, and Original Documents demonstrating the continuing existence of the Cooperativa (Ministry of Labor), and an original Document dated 2007 (Land Ministry) ratifying the Original Assignment of Title over the Property to the Cooperativa.
In response, the Alcalde (Mayor) claims that all the documents are false and in an outburst states, "We have the Authority"!
In the side-bar, the Alcalde changes his story, suggesting now that the Government owns the property - an apparent reference in support of the PGR filing now before the Judge.
