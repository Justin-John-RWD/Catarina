Austrian Development Corporation's Project Review

In 2007, during an ongoing property dispute, the Alcaldia of Catarina initiated a project to develop a new Mirador (lookout point) on contested land. The project was partially funded by the Austrian Development Corporation (ADC) with a grant of approximately US$200,000. The construction was documented as encroaching on private property subject to legal proceedings.

Justin Dobson, the property owner, raised concerns directly with ADC representatives, providing court-certified documents establishing his ownership of the land. In response, ADC's Nicaragua Country Director, Ms. Michaela Ellmeier, initiated a review process. However, the subsequent report relied heavily on verbal claims and did not address the documented court findings.

The report stated that the construction involved properties owned by the Alcaldia, but it failed to address the certified evidence showing the project's encroachment on private property. Despite the findings, ADC declined further discussions and did not respond to additional attempts at communication, even after a court ruling in favor of Mr. Dobson.

The project remains incomplete and abandoned, with concerns raised about the oversight and due diligence practices employed during the project's approval and funding.
