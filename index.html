Introduction & Overview

The purpose of this website is to document the history and current status of an ongoing land dispute in Catarina, Nicaragua. The site aims to provide a transparent and factual overview of the legal processes, actions taken, and challenges encountered since 2001.

Update - January 9, 2012

On November 18, 2011, the Appeals Court of Masaya issued a ruling overturning a 2010 lower court decision. The court directed the Public Registry to cancel the inscription of title transfer in the names of Justin Dobson and his wife. This marked a departure from two prior cases where the court had upheld their rights of dominion and possession.

Concerns regarding the ruling include the following points:

  • The application of a repealed law that had been superseded by new legislation.
  • The assertion that the Procurator General of the Republic represents the Municipality of Catarina, despite the autonomy of both entities.
  • The use of a specific, now-repealed law that did not pertain to Agrarian Reform titles.
  • The dismissal of prior cases and evidence, including a signed document from the Ministry of Property upholding the title.
  • Arguments about construction on the property, which had been funded by the Austrian Development Corporation, a funding source later withdrawn following an investigation.

This website aims to provide valuable information to those considering investment in Nicaragua or dealing with similar challenges. While the journey has been difficult, it demonstrates resilience and persistence in pursuing legal rights.
