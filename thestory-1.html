Location and Origin of Title

The property in question is located in the city of Catarina, approximately 500 meters north of the popular tourist destination "Mirador de Catarina," which offers a stunning view of Laguna de Apoyo.

Update - January 9, 2012

On November 18, 2011, the Appeals Court of Masaya issued a decision overturning a 2010 lower court ruling and instructed the Public Registry to cancel the inscription of title transfer in the names of Justin Dobson and his wife. This decision raised several legal and procedural concerns:

  • Application of a repealed and superseded law.
  • Assertion that the Procurator General represents the Municipality of Catarina, despite their separate autonomy.
  • Use of laws that do not pertain to Agrarian Reform titles.
  • Dismissal of prior cases and documented evidence supporting the title.
  • Arguments related to construction on the property funded by the Austrian Development Corporation.

The property was originally part of a larger estate, Finca TerraNica, confiscated by the Sandinista Government in 1981. In 1984, an Agrarian Reform Title was issued, assigning the property to the Cooperativa Felix Cruz Hernandez. The Ministry of Housing ratified this title in 2007 through its OOT office.

The title history includes two adjoining parcels, one of which was sold to the wife of Jaime Wheelock Roman, and later to a U.S. couple who developed the land. Their title has faced no disputes, underscoring the validity of the original title shared with the Cooperativa.
