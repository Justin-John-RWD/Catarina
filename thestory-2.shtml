Beginning of Conflict

In the late 1990's and early 2000, foreigners were showing a great deal of interest in property surrounding Laguna Apoyo and property values were rising. Members of the prominent local family, Gallegos, sought a way to "Cash in".

Initially, a Sr. Carlos Gallegos sought "Titulo Supletorio" over a 10.5 Mz lot used solely by peasant farmers. The application for title was brought before the district Judge in Masaya. Gallegos believed that the "ignorant farmers" would not contest. However, they did and they won. The application for "Titulo Supletorio" was denied on the basis that legal title was in fact held by the Cooperativa.

In 2001, Sr. Eddie Gallegos (cousin, perhaps?), the then Mayor of Catarina, initiated a new campaign to obtain the land - this time using the full authority of the local Council and his considerable Influence among the local agencies.

Sr. Eddie Gallegos presented, in the name of the Alcaldia, a "Declaracion Jurada" in which he solicited, from the Registrar, assignment of a new Finca for the same property. See more examples

As requested, against all normal procedure, and illegally, the Registrar complied with the request and opened a new entry in the registry with a new finca number (63447) in the name of the Alcaldia of Catarina, Feb 4, 2002. It should be noted that, in subsequent lawsuits, this document, the basis of the claim by the Alcaldia, was never presented in court - why?
The Cooperativa was subsequently forcibly removed from the property. They responded by initiating a lawsuit seeking the Cancellation of the fraudulent inscription by the Alcaldia.
