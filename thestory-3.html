Lawsuit to Cancel Alcaldia's Registration

The Cooperativa Felix Cruz Hernandez initiated a lawsuit to cancel the registration of property by the Alcaldia, asserting that the land was already titled and legally belonged to the Cooperativa.

During the lawsuit, the Alcaldia leveraged its position and influence:

  • Sr. Roman Cardoze Padilla, Delegate of the Office of Catastro in Masaya, issued an "aclaracion" on October 3, 2002. This document identified technical errors in the changes made to official property records by the Alcaldia, stating that the documentation presented was inappropriate. This "aclaracion" was submitted to the court by the Cooperativa as evidence.
  • Following this, Mayor Eddie Gallegos sought intervention from his cousin, Sr. Manuel Gallegos Lopez, the Director General of Catastro Nacional. On October 16, 2002, Manuel Gallegos issued a "Constancia General" emphasizing that changes to catastral records require adherence to proper procedures. This effectively prevented modifications to the existing records showing the Alcaldia as the owner.
  • As a consequence of issuing the "aclaracion," the delegate of Masaya Catastro was dismissed from his position.

To this day, the Alcaldia of Catarina maintains significant influence within the local office of Catastro Nacional, complicating efforts to resolve discrepancies in property records.
