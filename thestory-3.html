Lawsuit to Cancel Alcaldia's Fraudulent Registration

In defense of their Rights, Cooperativa Felix Cruz Hernandez commenced a law suit seeking cancellation of the Alcaldia's fraudulent registration - the simple point being that the land was already "titled" and the title belonged to them.

During this lawsuit, the Alcaldia displayed its Power and Influence:

When the Delegate of the Office of Catastro, Masaya, a Sr. Roman Cardoze Padilla issued an "aclaracion" dated Oct, 3, 2002, stating that there were technical errors committed in the recording of changes to official records relating to the subject property (on behalf of the Alcaldia of Catarina). He stated that the documentation presented by the Alcaldia were not appropriate. This "Aclaracion' was presented in the court case on behalf of the Cooperativa.

In reaction, the Mayor, Eddie Gallegos, sought assistance from his cousin, Sr. Manuel Gallegos Lopez, then Director General de Catastro Nacional. Manuel Gallegos emitted a Constancia General (Oct 16, 2002,) in which he specifies the policy of the Catastro General, that no changes can be made to catastral records without proper procedure. I.e. the (new) existing records of the Catastral (showing the Alcaldia as owner) cannot be changed. The delegate of Masaya Catastro was duly fired - payback for issuing the "aclaracion" against what was done in favor of the Alcaldia.


Until today, the Alcaldia of Catarina, through its Attorney, have significant influence within the local office of the Catastro Nacional and continue to "muddy" the data they hold.
